4  SOP: Server Transportation

4.1 Purpose

This SOP defines the standard procedure for safely transporting servers from the source location (vendor, guest house, or staging area) to the data center. It ensures that all servers arrive undamaged, properly documented, and ready for installation.

Lesson Learned

During our initial transportation from the guest house to the data center, one server was slightly damaged due to inadequate packaging. This SOP was created to prevent such incidents from ever recurring.

4.2 Scope

This procedure covers:

  • Transportation of HP DL385 branded servers
  • Transportation of assembled (non-branded) servers
  • Any movement of servers between locations (vendor ‚Üí guest house ‚Üí data center)
  • Both short-distance (within city) and long-distance (inter-city) transport

4.5 During Transportation

4.5.1 Loading Procedure

  1. Minimum 2 people for lifting any server (even small form factors)
  2. Lift from the bottom ‚Äî never grab by handles, faceplates, or cables
  3. Place on vehicle floor, never on seats or unsecured surfaces
  4. Secure with tie-down straps ‚Äî snug but not compressing the packaging
  5. Place ‚ÄúFragile‚Äù side facing up as marked
  6. Leave space between packages ‚Äî no crushing
  7. Take a photograph of the loaded vehicle interior

4.5.2 Transit Rules

  • Driver must be informed that cargo is fragile electronics
  • Maximum speed: 40 km/h on rough roads, 80 km/h on highways
  • Avoid sudden braking and sharp turns
  • If journey > 2 hours, check cargo at rest stop
  • No smoking in or near the vehicle
  • At least one team member rides with the cargo
  • Contact Team Lead if any incident occurs during transit

4.5.3 Route Planning

Route Planning Considerations
Factor Consideration

4.8 Server Type Reference

4.8.1 HP ProLiant DL385

  • Form Factor: 2U rack server
  • Weight: ~22-30 kg depending on configuration
  • Handling: Use original HP packaging when available
  • Key Vulnerability: Front bezel clips, drive bays
  • Special Note: iLO port is fragile ‚Äî protect during transport

4.8.2 Assembled Servers

  • Form Factor: Varies (typically 2U-4U)
  • Weight: Varies (15-35 kg typical)
  • Handling: Requires custom packaging ‚Äî original packaging unlikely to exist
  • Key Vulnerability: PCIe cards (GPU, NIC), unsecured drive cages, loose cables
  • Special Note: May not conform to standard rack dimensions ‚Äî verify before transport if it will fit the destination rack
Critical Reminder ‚Äî Assembled Servers

We experienced fitting issues with assembled servers in our racks. Before transporting assembled servers, confirm rack compatibility by checking:

  1. Server width, depth, and height match rack specifications
  2. Rail kits (if any) are compatible
  3. If no rail kits, shelf dimensions are verified

4.9 Record Keeping

All transportation records must include:

  1. Transportation ticket number
  2. Pre-transport photos (all 6 angles)
  3. Post-transport photos (all 6 angles + packaging state)
  4. Shock and moisture indicator readings
  5. Maker-Checker sign-off sheets (pre and post)
  6. Condition classification
  7. Any issue tickets filed
  8. RCA reports (if applicable)

Retention: All records must be kept for minimum 2 years.
