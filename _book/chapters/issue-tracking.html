10  Issue Tracking & Ticketing

10.1 Purpose

Every issue, deviation, or improvement opportunity must be captured in a ticket. Our ticketing system is the single source of truth for what happened, what‚Äôs being done about it, and what was learned.

10.2 Why Track Issues?

  • Nothing falls through the cracks ‚Äî Every issue gets attention
  • Accountability ‚Äî Every ticket has an owner
  • Transparency ‚Äî Anyone can see the status of any issue
  • Trends ‚Äî We can spot patterns and systemic problems
  • Audit trail ‚Äî Full history of actions taken
  • Learning ‚Äî Issues become training material

10.8 Ticket Management Best Practices

10.8.1 For Reporters

  1. Create tickets immediately ‚Äî don‚Äôt wait until end of day
  2. Be specific ‚Äî ‚ÄúServer damaged‚Äù is not enough; describe the damage
  3. Attach photos ‚Äî A picture is worth a thousand words
  4. Use the template ‚Äî It ensures you don‚Äôt forget important details
  5. Follow up ‚Äî Check your tickets regularly

10.8.2 For Assignees

  1. Acknowledge quickly ‚Äî Move to ‚ÄúIn Progress‚Äù when you start
  2. Update regularly ‚Äî Add comments as you work on it
  3. Don‚Äôt close silently ‚Äî Add a resolution comment before closing
  4. Link related tickets ‚Äî If issues are connected, link them
  5. Trigger RCA if needed ‚Äî Don‚Äôt wait to be told

10.8.3 For Team Lead

  1. Review all new tickets daily ‚Äî During or before standup
  2. Ensure proper prioritization ‚Äî Adjust if needed
  3. Track SLAs ‚Äî Are we responding and resolving on time?
  4. Spot trends ‚Äî Are the same types of issues recurring?
  5. Close the loop ‚Äî Ensure RCA actions are completed
